Avicularia alata

Version 1.1

This is a slightly updated version of my winged taratula. The only real difference is the abdomen of the spider, the opisthosoma, which now has a more hairy texture. I also tried to get a better pose compared to the previous fold.

    Paper 47cm square of double tissue
    Time spent ~9 hours
    Grid 52x52

    Version 1.0

    The genus 'Avicularia' contains several species of tarantulas and 'alata' is Latin and means 'winged'. So the name of this design basically means winged tarantula, but that just doesn't sound as fancy. But don't worry, fortunately these monsters don't exist. Anyway, I really like how this model turned out. The design process was much shorter than I expected. It's a pretty straightforward box pleated design, not too much to precrease (still not particularly easy to collapse or shape)

      Paper 46cm square of double tissue
      Time spent ~6.5 hours
